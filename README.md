# Customer Segmentation using RFM Analysis

## Project Overview

Customer segmentation is one of the most important strategies used by businesses to understand customer behavior, improve retention, increase revenue, and optimize marketing campaigns. This project applies the RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) framework to segment customers based on their purchasing patterns and identify high-value customer groups.

The project simulates a real-world customer analytics use case commonly performed by CRM teams, marketing departments, loyalty program managers, customer success teams, and business leadership in retail and e-commerce organizations.

---

# Objective

The primary objective of this project is to analyze customer purchasing behavior using the RFM framework and classify customers into meaningful segments that support data-driven business decisions.

The project aims to:

* Identify high-value customers
* Detect customers at risk of churn
* Improve customer retention strategies
* Enhance marketing effectiveness
* Increase customer lifetime value (CLV)
* Support personalized customer engagement initiatives

---

# Business Problem

Businesses often serve thousands of customers, but not every customer contributes equally to revenue and profitability.

Without proper segmentation, companies face challenges such as:

* Inefficient marketing campaigns
* Poor customer retention
* High customer acquisition costs
* Reduced customer loyalty
* Difficulty identifying valuable customers

Management requires a structured approach to understand customer behavior and allocate marketing resources effectively. RFM Analysis provides a proven method for solving this challenge by segmenting customers based on purchasing activity and spending patterns.

# RFM Framework

RFM stands for:

### Recency (R)

Measures how recently a customer made a purchase.

* Lower days since purchase indicate higher engagement.
* Recent customers are more likely to purchase again.

### Frequency (F)

Measures how often a customer makes purchases.

* Higher purchase frequency indicates stronger customer loyalty.

### Monetary (M)

Measures how much money a customer spends.

* Customers with higher spending contribute more revenue.

### RFM Scoring

Customers are assigned scores from 1 to 5 for each metric.

Example:

| Customer   | R | F | M |
| ---------- | - | - | - |
| Customer A | 5 | 5 | 5 |
| Customer B | 2 | 3 | 1 |

Higher overall RFM scores indicate more valuable customers.
